Dr. Miguel Sanz Jiménez

About

Hi there! My name is Miguel Sanz-Jiménez and I graduated in English Studies (First Class Honors) at Complutense University of Madrid (UCM). After that, I got a scholarship to study Contemporary American Literature at Loyola University Chicago and then I worked as a Culture and Language Assistant in West Liberty, Iowa, taking part in the Ministry of Education’s program. Meanwhile, I also taught English as a Second Language to adults in Muscatine Community College.


Back in Spain, I went to grad school, worked as an intern at UCM's Department of English Language & Linguistics, and I got an MA in Literary Translation. Now I am pursuing my PhD in English Linguistics. My dissertation deals with the translation of dialect features in literature and the ideological implications of translators’ strategies in the way characters with marked speeches are portrayed, particularly in the case of American slave narratives. Thus, it could be said my main areas of research are Translation Studies and Contrastive Linguistics.
